🔍 Key Findings

texaspropertyhelp.com has a solid technical foundation: HTTPS active, clean robots.txt, sitemap.xml with 44 URLs, logical URL structure, and good guide content (the hail damage checklist at 3,100 words is exceptional). The site is already appearing in Google with 3 impressions and the query "storm damage claim help" — early signal that the keyword strategy is working.

The biggest blocker right now is the complete absence of JSON-LD structured data. No page has schema markup — no LocalBusiness, no Article, no FAQPage, no Service schema. For YMYL (Your Money Your Life) content like insurance claims and contractor referrals, Google applies extra scrutiny. Schema signals trustworthiness and authority. This single fix could dramatically improve CTR and ranking potential across all pages.

The second major issue is city page thin content. Dallas at ~425 words, Houston at ~650 words — these cannot outrank established local competitors with 1,500–3,000 word pages. Each city page needs expansion with local-specific storm data, neighborhoods, ZIP codes, and service area details.

Unique strengths: Community Q&A section (zero competitors have this), bilingual Spanish content, 24/7 storm monitoring, multi-service coverage (roofing + HVAC + financing in one place), and a growing guide library. The hail damage checklist (3,100 words) is likely to become the site's strongest ranking asset.

🚨 Schema Markup — 0% Coverage

No page on texaspropertyhelp.com has JSON-LD structured data. This means: no rich snippets in Google, no FAQ dropdowns, no HowTo steps, no Article authorship, no LocalBusiness knowledge panel. For YMYL (insurance, contractor referrals) content, schema is not optional — it's how Google verifies your content's credibility.

1. LocalBusiness Schema — Add to homepage src/app/(en)/page.tsx
{ "@context": "https://schema.org", "@type": "LocalBusiness", "name": "Texas Property Help", "url": "https://texaspropertyhelp.com", "description": "Homeowner assistance and contractor referral platform for storm damage, roofing, HVAC, and insurance claims across Texas.", "areaServed": { "@type": "State", "name": "Texas" }, "serviceType": ["Storm Damage Repair", "Roofing", "HVAC", "Insurance Claim Assistance"], "knowsAbout": ["Hail damage", "Wind damage", "Roof insurance claims", "TDLR licensed contractors"], "sameAs": ["https://www.reddit.com/user/Plus_Air_5991/"] }
2. Article Schema — Add to every guide page
{ "@context": "https://schema.org", "@type": "Article", "headline": "Texas Hail Damage Homeowner Checklist: What to Do From Hour 1 to Claim Settled", "description": "Complete step-by-step guide for Texas homeowners after hail damage...", "datePublished": "2026-05-16", "dateModified": "2026-06-03", "author": { "@type": "Organization", "name": "Texas Property Help", "url": "https://texaspropertyhelp.com" }, "publisher": { "@type": "Organization", "name": "Texas Property Help", "url": "https://texaspropertyhelp.com" } }
3. FAQPage Schema — Add to all pages with FAQ sections
{ "@context": "https://schema.org", "@type": "FAQPage", "mainEntity": [{ "@type": "Question", "name": "Do I need to call my insurance before the roofer?", "acceptedAnswer": { "@type": "Answer", "text": "Call a roofer first. Have a local contractor inspect and document the damage before filing a claim..." } }, { "@type": "Question", "name": "How long do I have to file a hail claim in Texas?", "acceptedAnswer": { "@type": "Answer", "text": "Texas law gives you 1 year from the date of the storm to file a roof damage insurance claim..." } }] }
📱 Open Graph Tags — Add to all pages

Reddit is your #1 traffic source. Every time you post or someone shares a link, the preview appears blank or with a poor auto-generated description. Fix this by adding OG metadata in Next.js:

// In src/app/(en)/page.tsx metadata export: export const metadata: Metadata = { title: "Storm Damage Help for Texas Homeowners | Texas Property Help", description: "Get connected with vetted, TDLR-licensed Texas contractors...", openGraph: { title: "Storm Damage Help for Texas Homeowners | Texas Property Help", description: "Free guidance and vetted contractor referrals after hail, wind, or water damage across Texas.", url: "https://texaspropertyhelp.com", siteName: "Texas Property Help", images: [{ url: "https://texaspropertyhelp.com/images/home-hero.jpg", width: 1200, height: 630, alt: "Texas storm damage help - roofing and insurance claim guidance" }], locale: "en_US", type: "website", }, twitter: { card: "summary_large_image", title: "Storm Damage Help for Texas Homeowners", description: "Free guidance and vetted contractor referrals after hail, wind, or water damage across Texas.", images: ["https://texaspropertyhelp.com/images/home-hero.jpg"], }, };

Replicate this pattern for every service page and guide page with page-specific titles, descriptions, and images.
